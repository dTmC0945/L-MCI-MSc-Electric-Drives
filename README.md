# Introduction

Welcome to the lecture materials for the course, M.Sc. Drive systems, held at MCI IV. This lecture covers a lot of topics
related to electric machines, from their construction to how they are controlled. Due to the nature of industrial relevance
Only AC drives will be covered.

> [!NOTE]
> This does not mean DC drives are irrelevant. On the contrary they play a pivotal role in many parts of the industry.
> However, due to the limitation of the lecture time frame this topic will be omitted from the lecture.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This lecture is two (2) SWS and has **no assignments**. However, there will be an exam.

ALl lecture materials for this course will be published here. So please check here for most up-to-date material.

# Table of Contents

The lecture will cover the following points.

  1. Fundamentals of Power Engineering.
     - Three Phase systems (i.e., wye, delta connections)
     - Three-phase waveforms
     - Complex numbers
     - Apparent, Real and Reactive power
     - Creating a Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF)
  2. Induction Drives
     - Rotor types
     - Stator construction
     - Operation principle
     - The concept of slip and why it is necessary
     - Squirrel-cage rotor design
     - Equivalent Circuit
     - Torque-speed characteristics
     - Induction motor starting methods
  3. Linear Induction Drives
     - Construction
     - The concept of thrust
     - The goodness factor
     - Applications 
  4. Magnetic Levitation
     - Single Phase Induction Drives
     - Operation Principle
     - Types of Single phase Drives
     - Shaded-pole induction drive
  5. Dynamic Modelling of Induction Drives
     - Real-time modelling of a two-phase
     - Transformations to obtain constant matrices
     - 3 to 2 phase transformation
     - Per Unit (p.u.) analysis
     - Dynamic simulation of an Induction Drive
  6. Control of Induction Drives
     - Armature frequency control 
     - Line Control
     - Rotor - Resistance Control
     - Field-Operated-Control (FOC) Technique
